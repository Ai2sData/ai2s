From Innovation to Scale (I2S)

In recent years, the AI community has witnessed an exciting acceleration in innovation across foundation models, deep learning, and new AI applications across numerous verticals. AI innovations driven by both academic and industry research labs have rapidly been adopted by big tech companies and startups to deliver value-differentiated products and services.

For many machine learning researchers looking to commercialize their work, a frequently wondered question is, “How do I kickstart a startup that can commercialize my research innovations?”. ML practitioners in the KDD community are curious about how big tech and startups scale AI research and innovations to be used by millions of users. With rapid advances in generative AI algorithms, the landscape is evolving around the opportunities and challenges of building robust and scalable generative solutions.

The second edition of this interactive workshop aims to build on this discourse focusing on two aspects: First, bringing together invited AI thought leaders from academia, big tech, and startups to share their perspective on realizing the opportunities of GenAI in various business verticals via use-case themes, challenges, and risks. Second, inviting startup founders (from academia and industry) focused on verticalized GenAI offerings to share their journey in product commercialization and the challenges of the GenAI productization landscape.

Topics of interest in this workshop include but are not limited to:

  • Commercializing AI research innovations
  • Scaling AI startups
  • Acquiring customers in challenging market conditions
  • Building successful teams
  • Pitching for initial funding
  • Developing and executing go-to-market strategies
  • Best practices for AI innovation

If you have valuable insights and experiences to share in any of these areas, we encourage you to submit a proposal to speak at the I2S workshop. We welcome submissions from AI thought leaders, startup founders, and other professionals with relevant expertise.

Spotlight Speakers

Sofus Macskassy

Founder, Zaimler AI

Speaker Bio:
Sofus Macskassy is the Co-Founder and Chief Scientist of a startup focused on leveraging AI to simplify getting your data AI ready. Prior to his current role, Sofus was a Director of Engineering and AI at LinkedIn where he innovated on automating taxonomy and knowledge graph creation. He was the VP of Data and AI at two startups and was one of the founders of the Applied ML team at Facebook. He has a PhD in AI and has been an active researcher in AI for over two decades, focusing on graphs, text mining, information extraction and rigorous evaluation of ML models.

Ben Cistecky

Managing Partner, Resaro AI

Speaker Bio:
Ben is an investor in Resaro, a company established by Temasek, and together with the Resaro team shapes the future of AI safety and assurance. He spent 11 years with Temasek where he spearheaded successful investments in Fintech and played a key role in establishing the AI team in 2019. Ben holds a degree in business from INSEAD.
Ben is a governing committee member of the AI Verify Foundation, which promotes open-source methods and tools to enable responsible adoption of AI. He also serves on the Singapore Computer Society's AI Ethics and Professional certification program committee.

Panel 1: Gen AI - implications for ventures, product development, and go-to-market

Generative AI has changed the world and how the general public perceives the promise of Artificial Intelligence. However, it also has far reaching implications for early-stage AI ventures themselves. As compared to “traditional” AI algorithms, Gen AI has changed the way we train models, the amount of capital it takes to procure compute resources, and how to establish a safe and responsible way of using LLMs. Responsible AI and impending regulation across different jurisdictions are on everyone’s mind. Our panel of AI founders will explore the impact that Gen AI has on their companies, products and services.

  • 4:30pm – VC fund spotlight 1: Arnaud Barthelemy, Alpha Intelligence Capital

Panel 2: Thoughts from early stage investors: Raising capital for your venture in the GenAI era

Generative AI has brought a renewed focus on the promise of AI across a variety of enterprises and has accelerated the expectations on the paradigmatic shift. The world has seen rapid development in Gen AI models – both general-purpose, and domain-specific. However, building Gen AI ventures is significantly different from classical AI offerings owing to the difficulty in establishing business models, differences in the product economics, evolving safety and regulatory concerns, difficulty in evaluating and validating the products, concerns around reliability, and so on. Consequently, realizing ROI from GenAI is proving to be non-trivial. This panel will focus on the perspectives of investors on the evolving Gen AI space, realizable business opportunities, and takeaways for existing and aspiring early-stage ventures and founders for building successful products and offerings.

  •  4:50pm -5:50pm Panel discussion moderated by Mohak Shah, Praescivi Advisors
  • Panelist:  Arnaud Barthelemy, Michael Zeller
